  1. Symptoms of COVID-19

The following symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure:

  • Fever 
  • Cough 
  • Shortness of Breath
  • Sore Throat

  1. How to Protect Yourself

Other ways you can protect yourself:  

  • If you cannot wash, use hand sanitizer with greater than 60% alcohol until you have access to soap and water.
  • Clean and sanitize common areas (door knobs, light switches, counter tops, desks).
  • Clean and sanitize commonly used objects (cell phones, remotes, laptops).
  • Consider limiting travel, especially by plane or cruise.
  • Use of these types of products for environmental cleaning. 
  • Stay home or at place of residence, except as needed to maintain essential needs.
  1. Social Distancing

Social distancing is a practice recommended by public health officials to stop or slow down the spread of contagious diseases.  The key is to minimize the number of gathering as much as possible and achieve space between individuals when events or activities cannot be modified, postponed, or canceled.  

6 feet is the advised distance between you and another person when practicing social distancing.  

Who is at higher risk?

  •  Older adults 65 years and older

  • Individuals with compromised immune systems

  • Individuals with chronic medical conditions, such as heart disease, lung disease, and diabetes

What to do if you believe you have COVID-19:

  • Call your health care provider for direction. They will ask you some important questions to determine what to do next
