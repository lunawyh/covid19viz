Posted on: April 11, 2020

Update on COVID-19 (Novel Coronavirus)

April 10, 2020 - See Contra Costa Regional Health Foundation Press Release (PDF): Fund to Bring Emergency Relief to Bay Area Residents Impacted by COVIDâ€19.

April 10, 2020 - See Contra Costa Health Services Press Release: Contra Costa County Fairgrounds in Antioch to be Converted into Potential Care Site for COVID-19 Patients.

April 10, 2020 - See Contra Costa Health Services Press Release: Contra Costa Reminds Residents to Observe Religious Practices at Home this Weekend.

April 7, 2020 –Contra Costa Joins Bay Area Counties in Extending Distance Learning Through End of the 2019-2020 School Year. See Contra Costa County Office of Education Press Release (PDF).

April 3, 2020 - See Press Release (PDF): Contra Costa Issues Mass Isolation and Quarantine Orders for People with COVID-19 and Their Close Contacts. See Mass Quarantine Order (PDF) and Mass Isolation Order (PDF).

April 3, 2020 - See Press Release: Contra Costa Health Confirms COVID-19 Outbreak at a Senior Facility. Health Services press conference replay is on YouTube.

April 2, 2020 - See New Guidance on Masking (PDF): New Bay Area Public Health Recommendation to Cover Your Face.  

April 2, 2020 – See Press Release: Craneway Pavilion in Richmond to be Converted into a Federal Medical Station for COVID-19 Patients.

March 31, 2020 – See Press Release (PDF): Bay Area Health Officers Issue Updated Stay-at-Home Order with New Restrictions to Last Through May 3, 2020. According to the release, more time and additional restrictions are needed to slow the spread and reduce future impact on local hospitals from COVID-19. See Health Officer Order Through May 3 (PDF). See Appendix A: Social Distancing Protocol (PDF). Read the Summary of New Shelter Order (PDF) for further clarification. For more information and FAQs, visit the Health Services website.

As of March 17, 2020 – County offices may have limited public access to its facilities, as it follows the County Health Officer Order that is intended to protect lives. See Update on County Offices and Services.

See Answers to Frequently Asked Questions in English and Spanish. If you have questions about the Health Order, you can reach our Call Center in English or Spanish at 1-844-729-8410, 8 AM - 5 PM. For assistance after hours in multiple languages, please call 211 or 800-833-2900 or text HOPE to 20121.

Visit cchealth.org/coronavirus for latest updates. Follow Contra Costa Health Services on Twitter @CoCoHealth or CCHS Facebook page. Follow the County @CCCounty. (You do not need a Twitter account to see postings.) See all CCHS videos related to COVID-19.

RESOURCES - LOCAL

  • See Emergency Relief Fund (PDF). You may be eligible. For more information and to apply, call (925) 521-5065.
  • See list of Emergency Food Resources (PDF) in Contra Costa County.
  • Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) helps families with food and support services. You may qualify for and receive support quickly. See details in News Release. Visit cchealth.org/wic or call 1 (800) 414-4WIC.
  • For seniors, learn how to Stay Connected (PDF) and find Community Resources for Seniors.
  • For information about schools, check with your school district or visit County Office of Education. (See also curriculum resources).
  • For social services information, including resources and support, visit EHSD.org.
  • Unemployment and Economic information and resources for Businesses & Employees.
  • For information about CalWORKS, CalFresh, or Medi-Cal, visit Employment & Human Services.
  • Property Tax Delinquent Deadline was April 10. For information to assist you, visit www.cctax.us.
  • Report cases of price gouging. Email the County District Attorney’s Consumer Protection Unit.
  • Call Health Services’ Call Center at 1-844-729-8410, 8 am to 5 pm, daily in English or Spanish.
  • Call 2-1-1, if you need information and assistance.

RESOURCES - STATE & FEDERAL

  • State of California has a new COVID-19 website with links to many resources.
  • Have you lost your job? See California’s one-stop resource for people impacted by job loss during the pandemic.
  • See the California’s Volunteers website to learn how you can get involved.
  • To apply for Unemployment Insurance, visit the California Employment and Development Department: www.edd.ca.gov/unemployment/.
  • Find information about applying for disability or unemployment and if you may qualify for Paid Family Leave.
  • See the CA Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) for updates and service options.
  • For Federal Income Tax information, visit the Internal Revenue Service at IRS.gov. Check for Tax Deadline Extended, Tax Relief, and more.
  • For State Income Tax information, visit CA Department of Tax and Fee Administration.
  • For resources to avoid scams and protect yourself financially, visit official government websites:
    • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) has resources to protect and manage your finances, including how to avoid debt collection scams.
    • Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has information on avoiding scams.
    • Federal Trade Commission (FTC) on how to avoid coronavirus scams.
  • Find more health information in multiple languages at California Department of Public Health. 
  • Get further information and guidance on coronavirus at Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
  • If you or someone you know communicates using American Sign Language, watch videos on COVID-19 on the CDC #ASL Health YouTube channel.
